A Legacy Born from Love

In 2012, Chef Mohan Suryavanshi opened the doors to what would become Mumbai's most cherished dining destination. Growing up in a small village in Gujarat, Mohan learned the secrets of authentic Indian cooking from his grandmother, who believed that food was the purest expression of love.

Today, THE BOYS Restaurant serves over 300 families every week, each meal prepared with the same care and attention that Mohan's grandmother taught him decades ago. Our spices come from trusted farms in Kerala, our paneer is handmade fresh each morning, and every recipe honors the traditions of our ancestors.

What started as a humble 12-table eatery has grown into a beloved institution, but our heart remains the same: to share the warmth of Indian hospitality and the richness of our culinary heritage with every guest.

"In our kitchen, every spice tells a story, and every meal creates a memory."
— Chef Mohan Suryavanshi

Chef's Special

Misal pav - The Best Dish

This week, Chef Mohan presents his signature Misal pav, Dive into the authentic taste of Misal Pav, Maharashtra's most beloved street food. This iconic dish features a spicy sprouted bean curry (Usal) and a fiery red gravy (Rassa), all topped with crunchy farsan, onions, and coriander. Served with soft pav, it's a perfect explosion of flavor and texture. Follow our easy, step-by-step recipe to bring this classic to your kitchen!

Kolhapuri Misal: This is the most famous and is known for being extremely spicy and fiery red. It's not for the faint of heart. It is often served without the usal (sprouts) in the main gravy, which is added separately. Puneri Misal: This version is typically milder and slightly sweeter than the Kolhapuri one. A unique thing about Puneri misal is that it is often served with pohe (flattened rice) at the bottom of the bowl, under the sprouts. Nashik Misal: This version has a unique flavor because it often uses a Kala Masala (black spice mix), giving the rassa (gravy) a darker color and a smoky, distinct taste. It's often served with a specific type of crispy papad. Khandeshi Misal: Also from North Maharashtra, this version is known for its intense heat and use of black masala, similar to the Nashik style.

Our authentic Misal Pav is served with soft pav, a generous topping of crunchy farsan, freshly chopped onions, and a lemon wedge. A side of cooling dahi (yogurt) is included to perfectly balance the spice.
